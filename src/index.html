But What Lies Beneath?

Despite its massive scale, the pharmaceutical industry's inner geography remains largely unknown.

Where are its decision-making cores? Which cities produce, innovate, or distribute? How are these hubs linked?

COVID-19 exposed the system's fragility ‚Äî fragmented supply chains, sudden shortages, and critical dependencies.

To build resilience, we must first uncover its spatial architecture.

From Trade to Control, Towards Resilience

Our project unpacks the global pharmaceutical system by progressing from macro-level trade patterns to the micro-dynamics of control and resilience.

We begin with analysing international trade structures, then map out city-level functional roles, uncover corporate control hierarchies, and finally evaluate systemic resilience through spatial metrics.

These four modules build a layered understanding of how pharmaceutical power and vulnerability are distributed across the urban network.

What is Urban Resilience Index?

When the cities we live in face shocks such as global epidemics, climate change, and economic crises, some cities are able to adapt quickly and grow steadily, while others are vulnerable to severe impacts. The Urban Resilience Index (URI) is a comprehensive measure of a city's ability to stabilise, recover and continue to function in such ‚Äúvolatile environments‚Äù.

  • Functional Diversity(FD)
  • Operating Revenue(OP)
  • ESG Index

What is ESG index?

    ESG index is now an important criterion used globally to judge whether a business is responsible and sustainable.

  • Environmental(E): whether a company attaches importance to green development and pollution reduction
  • Social(S): whether the treatment of employees, customer relations, and community impact are good
  • Governance(G): whether the management is transparent and the system is sound
  • Overall(O): A composite score

environmentally orientated city

environmentally friendly, energy efficient and carbon reducing; and have a strong ability to adapt to climate policies

Socially orientated city

perform well in terms of employee well-being and community responsibility; place more emphasis on social equity and public relations; and are more likely to gain social support and trust in the event of a public incident

Governance-oriented city

transparent and compliant in their management; have well-developed systems and clear response mechanisms; and are more able to respond quickly to emergencies and allocate resources efficiently
